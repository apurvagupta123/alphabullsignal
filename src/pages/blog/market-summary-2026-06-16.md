## The Big Picture

Indian markets were rangebound today, not because of bad news — but because the good news had already been priced in. Monday's sharp 1% rally, powered by the US-Iran peace deal and crashing crude oil prices, left the market in a "digest and consolidate" mood on Tuesday. Add weekly F&O expiry into the mix, and you get exactly what we saw: an early morning push toward 24,000 on Nifty, followed by a slow fade back as traders squared positions. The market closed roughly flat from Monday, but the macro backdrop has genuinely improved.

## The Story Behind the Move

**1. The Iran factor is real — and big for India**

The US and Iran announced a peace agreement last week, with a formal signing scheduled for June 19 in Switzerland. This ended a 107-day conflict that had pushed crude above $100/bbl, spiked global inflation fears, and kept the Strait of Hormuz under constant threat. When the deal was confirmed, Brent crude fell nearly 5% in a single session to around $83/bbl. For India — which imports over 80% of its oil — this is significant. Lower crude means a smaller import bill, less pressure on the current account deficit, and room for inflation to cool.

**2. Rupee on the move**

The rupee strengthened sharply on Monday, touching ₹94.95 intraday before closing around ₹95.11. That might seem like a small move, but a rupee gaining 70 paise in a day is noteworthy. Cheaper oil and reduced FII selling pressure are both tailwinds. For context, just last week the rupee was struggling near ₹95.57 on the back of rising crude. The narrative has flipped.

**3. Weekly expiry added noise**

Tuesday is weekly options expiry day on NSE. This creates mechanical buying and selling as traders unwind their positions, which explains why markets were volatile intraday without a clear directional catalyst. The India VIX fell to 14.35, signalling that overall market fear is subsiding — a quiet positive.

## What to Watch Tomorrow

- **Brent crude price action**: If oil continues to slip toward $80, India's macro story gets even cleaner. Any reversal on peace deal doubts could quickly reverse gains.
- **FII flows**: Foreign investors had been net sellers in recent weeks due to Middle East uncertainty. Watch whether Monday's tentative slowdown in FII selling continues into a buying streak.

---

## Investor Takeaway

The US-Iran peace deal has quietly handed Indian investors a gift — falling crude oil is one of the most powerful positive macro signals for India, and right now the market hasn't fully priced in what sustained $80 oil means for corporate margins, inflation, and RBI policy. If you have been waiting on the sidelines, the macro setup is improving.
