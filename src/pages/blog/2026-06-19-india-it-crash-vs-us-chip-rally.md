If you looked at Indian markets and US markets today, you'd think they were living on different planets.

**NIFTY 50** slipped 199 points (-0.82%) to **23,969**. **SENSEX** dropped 787 points (-1.02%) to **76,623**. Routine enough. But beneath the surface, India's IT sector was in freefall — **NIFTY IT crashed 5.72%** in a single session, one of its sharpest single-day drops of the year.

Meanwhile, across the ocean, the NASDAQ was celebrating. Up **1.91%** to **26,518**. The S&P 500 climbed **1.08%** to **7,501**. Intel — yes, the same company that's been struggling for years — surged **10.64%** in a single day.

Same industry. Completely opposite directions. What's going on?

---

## The Indian IT Bloodbath

The damage in Indian IT was broad and brutal:

- **Infosys**: ₹1,035.70, down **-8.14%**
- **TCS**: ₹2,070.50, down **-6.03%**
- **Mphasis**: ₹2,195.10, down **-6.06%**
- **HCL Technologies**: ₹1,103.20, down **-5.04%**
- **LatentView Analytics**: ₹294.45, down **-4.80%**
- **Persistent Systems**: ₹4,715, down **-4.56%**
- **Wipro**: ₹176.15, down **-3.66%**
- **Coforge**: ₹1,438, down **-3.03%**

This wasn't one or two stocks having a bad day. The entire sector got hit — large caps, mid caps, analytics plays, everything.

**India VIX** — the fear gauge — jumped 4.99% to 13.3, signalling that traders are bracing for more volatility.

---

## What the Money Flow Tells Us

The institutional flow gives away the real story. **FIIs (Foreign Institutional Investors) sold a net ₹1,025 Cr** on the day. That's foreign money walking out. But here's what's interesting — **DIIs (Domestic Institutional Investors) bought a net ₹3,517 Cr**, absorbing the selling and keeping the broader market from a deeper fall.

DIIs buying aggressively while FIIs exit is often a sign that domestic money sees value — but it also means global investors are re-pricing Indian IT against a changing landscape.

---

## The US Side: Chips Are Having a Moment

While Indian IT fell, the US chip and semiconductor space was on fire:

- **Intel**: +10.64% to $134 — a massive single-day move
- **Qualcomm**: +6.17% to $226
- **AMD**: +4.86% to $537
- **NVIDIA**: +2.95% to $210.69
- **Amazon**: +2.90% | **Meta**: +1.70% | **Alphabet**: +1.17%

The divergence makes sense when you frame it this way: the US tech rally is **hardware and AI infrastructure** led — chips, compute, data centres. Indian IT, by contrast, is **services and outsourcing** led — writing code for US and European clients.

If US companies are investing more in AI chips and automation, the logical follow-on question is: how much less will they spend on outsourcing software development to Indian IT firms? That's the fear the market is pricing in today.

---

## The Broader Picture

The rest of Indian markets held up reasonably well. **NIFTY PHARMA** was actually up +0.18%. **Reliance** gained +0.38%. **ICICI Bank** edged up +0.29%. **Bharti Airtel** added +0.52%.

The pain was concentrated in IT — which is almost a relief, because it means this is a sector-specific re-rating, not a market-wide panic.

**NIFTY BANK** was down only -0.63%, **NIFTY MIDCAP 50** shed a modest -0.37%, and **NIFTY SMALLCAP** was flat.

---

## What to Watch

The next key question is whether this is a one-day flush or the beginning of a structural downgrade for Indian IT. Watch for:

1. **Earnings guidance** from TCS, Infosys, and HCL in the coming weeks — any revenue warnings will extend the pain
2. **FII flow** over the next 3–5 sessions — if selling continues, the pressure on IT won't lift
3. **INR/USD** — a weakening rupee historically benefits IT exporters, which could provide a floor
4. **US corporate IT budgets** — the ultimate driver; if US companies keep cutting software services spend, Indian IT faces a structural headwind

For now, the market has spoken loudly. India's IT sector needs a new story — and today's session made clear that the old one isn't enough anymore.
